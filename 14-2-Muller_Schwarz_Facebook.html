What do we learn from this?

  • When you turn off the internet or Facebook, the violence caused by AfD members plummets AND is no longer related to the number of angry facebook posts nationwide!

  • “To establish causality, we exploit exogenous variation in major internet and Facebook outages, which fully undo the correlation between social media and hate crime.”


Questions on this?

The authors argue that their test, and others we didn’t have time to get into today, support only some of their proposed mechanisms


They discard “information exchange” because the posts they reviewed showed that location information was only common in references to politicians and crimes that already happened

  • Not used to identify refugee targets


They discard “persuasion” because it seems wildly unlikely that someones’ xenophobia would turn off like a light switch when the internet goes out.

  • People’s attitudes do not change on a week-to-week basis


They find additional support for the coordination mechanism in one of the regressions that examines the number of perpetrators to a crime

  • Social media posts tend to produce crimes by groups of AfD members, not lone wolf attacks


They also find support for spillover attacks using a regression that focuses on crimes in neighboring municipalities

  • Just like the research on war being contagious, they find that AfD crimes tend to inspire neighboring municipality crimes

Bottom line, does this paper demonstrate a convincing link between facebook usage and terrorist incidents? Why or why not?
